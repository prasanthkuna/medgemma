# Aegis Health - Surgical Medical Necessity Policy (S-104)
## Topic: Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA)

### 1. Clinical Indications
Total Knee Arthroplasty is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria are met:

- **1.1 Radiographic Evidence**: X-ray documentation of Kellgren-Lawrence Grade III or IV osteoarthritis (joint space narrowing, osteophytes).
- **1.2 Failed Conservative Management**: At least 3 months of documented failure of non-surgical treatment including:
    - Physical therapy (structured program).
    - Weight loss (if BMI > 30).
    - NSAIDs or other analgesic medications.
    - Intra-articular injections (optional but encouraged).
- **1.3 Functional Impairment**: Documented chronic pain restricting Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) despite conservative management.

### 2. Exclusion Criteria
TKA is NOT considered medically necessary if:
- Patient has active systemic or local infection.
- Conservative management (Physical Therapy) has not been attempted for at least 3 months.
- X-ray shows only Grade I or II osteoarthritis.
